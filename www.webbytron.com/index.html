What can webbytron do for me?

Create your own iOS and Android apps with just a few clicks and without any understanding of code. Use your mobile web app as the basis, and the intelligent webbytron app template wraps your web content into a real, native iOS or Android app! No more coding, no more plugins needed, it just works.

How will my app look like?

Your app will work as a full-screen browser. All the changes you have done on your website will be applied to your iOS & Android app immediately. Users will navigate your web app exactly as your web app on Safari for iOS/Chrome for Android without the browser interface. Please take a look at our example apps.

What do I need, what are the requirements?

webbytron is a 100% complete full app template. You would just require an existing web app or a website to convert into an app. Get started by just downloading the webbytron app template and continue to follow the extremely easy step-by-step documentation. For Android, the latest Android Studio app is required. For iOS, the latest Xcode app (running on your own Mac or MacInCloud) is needed. For an App Store or Play Store submission, a developer account from Apple and/or Google is required. No coding knowledge is required.

Do I need any coding skills?

No. webbytron is a 100%-ready app template, and the setup of your app works without any understanding of coding. Just insert your website address, and you are done. Documentation, free updates, and support are included.

Can I submit my app to the Apple App Store & Google Play Store? Do you offer an app publishing service?

Yes. webbytron comes with an Apple App Store/Google Play Store Approval Guarantee. 96,4% of all webbytron apps get approved by the Review Team in the first attempt. Please make sure to submit an app using a website/web app that behaves like a mobile app (for example, by using responsive design). Make sure that your content does not contradict the legal requirements of the Apple and Google guidelines. Will my app work offline?

If you provide a local HTML folder, yes. If your web content is hosted on a website, your app will display a beautiful Offline screen with a "Try again" button instead.
